---
title: "Things I'm learning to let be"
date: "2026-01-22"
description: "I used to believe that clarity was the goal."
---

I used to believe that clarity was the goal. That if I just thought hard enough, sat still long enough, wrote long enough — things would resolve into something I could hold and name and understand.

They don't, always. And I'm learning to be okay with that.

---

There are things in my life right now that I cannot figure out. Relationships that exist in a gray area I keep trying to color in. Decisions I've made and unmade so many times the edges have gone soft. Questions I've been carrying so long they've started to feel like personality traits.

I used to fight this. I used to treat unresolved things like emergencies, like the uncertainty itself was the problem. But lately I'm wondering if the urgency to resolve everything is what's actually getting in the way.

What if some things are just meant to be held loosely? What if *not knowing* is sometimes the most honest position you can be in?

---

I'm learning to let some things exist without a verdict. To say *I don't know yet* and mean it without apology. To trust that the clarity will come when it comes — or it won't, and I'll find a way to live with that too.

This is harder than it sounds. But it's also quieter. And I've been craving quiet.
