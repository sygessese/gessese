---
title: "On beginning again"
date: "2026-02-10"
description: "There is something quietly radical about starting over."
---

There is something quietly radical about starting over. Not the dramatic kind — not burning it all down — but the small, private act of deciding that the version of yourself you were yesterday doesn't have to be the ceiling.

I've been thinking about this a lot lately. About how we treat beginnings as though they require permission. As though there's a committee somewhere that decides when you've suffered enough, learned enough, waited long enough to try again.

There isn't. There's just you, and the next thing, and the choice to move toward it anyway.

---

I used to think starting over meant admitting failure. That every time I reset, I was proving something about my inability to commit, to follow through, to *be* the kind of person who finishes things. But I'm learning — slowly, the way you learn things that matter — that beginning again is its own kind of finishing. That you can complete a chapter without having reached the end of the book.

The version of me that lived inside the last beginning taught me things I couldn't have learned any other way. I'm not abandoning her when I move forward. I'm carrying her.

---

So if you're standing at the edge of something new and waiting for a sign: this is it. Not because the timing is perfect or because you're ready — you're probably not, and that's fine. But because the only thing worse than beginning again is spending another year not beginning at all.
